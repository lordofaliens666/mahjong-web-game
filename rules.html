Jade Parlour
PlayRules

How to Play

Jade Parlour plays a simplified version of Singapore-style mahjong. You sit East; three AI opponents fill South, West, and North.

Objective

Be the first to complete a winning hand: four sets (a triplet or a run of three) plus one pair, using 14 tiles total. You can win by drawing the final tile yourself (a self-draw) or by claiming someone else's discard.

The Tiles

  • Circles, Bamboo, Myriad — three suits, numbered 1–9, four copies each.
  • Winds — East, South, West, North, four copies each.
  • Dragons — Red, Green, White, four copies each.
  • Flowers — 8 bonus tiles. Drawing one doesn't count toward your hand — it's set aside and you immediately draw a replacement.

Turn Flow

On your turn you draw a tile, then discard one. Play passes East → South → West → North → East. After any discard, other players may interrupt the normal turn order to call it:

  • Pong — claim a discard to complete a triplet, if you hold a matching pair. Anyone can call it.
  • Kong — claim a discard to complete a foursome, if you hold three matching tiles. You immediately draw a replacement tile.
  • Chi — claim a discard to complete a run (sequence), if you hold two adjacent tiles. Only the player seated immediately after the discarder may call it.
  • Win — claim a discard (or your own draw) to complete your hand.

When more than one call is possible on the same discard, priority is Win > Kong > Pong > Chi.

Scoring (simplified)

Winning hands are scored in tai (points of value), then converted to game points. This is a simplified subset of official Singapore scoring rules — not the full rule book.

ConditionTai
Base hand1
Self-drawn win+1
Fully concealed hand (no calls made)+1
All triplets (no sequences)+3
Half flush (one suit + honor tiles)+3
Full flush (one suit only)+6
Each dragon triplet+1
Your own seat-wind triplet+1

Each tai is worth 10 points. On a self-drawn win, each opponent pays that amount. On a win off a discard, the discarder alone pays triple.

This is a single-player prototype built to explore the design — there's no real backend, accounts, or money involved. Bot names are randomized each round, and you can change the table theme and bot pace from Settings (the ⚙ icon).
