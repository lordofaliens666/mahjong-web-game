Jade Parlour
PlayRules

How to Play

Jade Parlour offers three rulesets, chosen in the Lobby before you sit down. You always sit East; three AI opponents fill South, West, and North.

The Tiles

  • Circles, Bamboo, Myriad — three suits, numbered 1–9, four copies each.
  • Winds — East, South, West, North, four copies each.
  • Dragons — Red, Green, White, four copies each.
  • Flowers — 8 bonus tiles. Drawing one doesn't count toward your hand — it's set aside and you immediately draw a replacement.
  • Jokers — American games only. 8 wildcard tiles that can substitute for any tile toward a hand pattern.

Turn Flow & Calling

On your turn you draw a tile, then discard one. Play passes East → South → West → North → East. After any discard, other players may interrupt the normal turn order to call it:

  • Pong — claim a discard to complete a triplet, if you hold a matching pair. Anyone can call it, in any ruleset.
  • Kong — claim a discard to complete a foursome, if you hold three matching tiles. You immediately draw a replacement tile. Singapore and Hong Kong only.
  • Chi — claim a discard to complete a run, if you hold two adjacent tiles. Only the player seated immediately after the discarder may call it. Singapore and Hong Kong only — American mahjong never allows chi; runs must be built from your own draws.
  • Win — claim a discard (or your own draw) to complete your hand.

When more than one call is possible on the same discard, priority is Win > Kong > Pong > Chi.

Scoring

Win shape: four sets (triplet or run) plus one pair, 14 tiles total. Scored in tai, no minimum required to win.

Same win shape as Singapore, but scored in fan — and you need at least 3 fan to legally declare a win at all (a complete-but-cheap hand must keep playing). Full flush is worth more than in Singapore; both your seat wind and the round wind (always East) can score, stacking to +2 for an East player holding an East-wind triplet.

A genuinely different game: instead of a generic 4-sets-and-a-pair shape, your 14 tiles must match one of the named patterns below exactly (an original, simplified set — not the real, copyrighted NMJL card). Jokers can substitute for any tile anywhere, including in the pair. Each pattern has a fixed point value.

This is a single-player prototype built to explore the design — there's no real backend, accounts, or money involved. Bot names are randomized each round, and you can change the table theme and bot pace from Settings (the ⚙ icon).
